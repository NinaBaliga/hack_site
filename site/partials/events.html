Past Events
The 2nd Annual Code for NoVA Hackathon (as part of the National Day of Civic Hacking) on May 31st was a huge success! Over 70 civic hackers worked on projects throughout the day to help make our communities better.
Throughout the day, many prominent people came by to see what this “hackathon was all about”. Including Alexandria’s CIO, Kevin Edwards, NSF’s Director and Deputy Director, Dr. France Cordova and Dr. Cora B. Merratt, and numerous local government officials.
Representatives from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), US Patent & Trade Office (USPTO), Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), Department of Energy (DOE), City of Alexandria, Arlington County, and Institute of Museum and Library Services (IMLS) presented data sets and challenges to the participants early in the morning.
The hackers then split up into groups based on their interests, and by 10am everyone was collaborating to come up with innovative new solutions to the problems at hand.
By the end of the night, 7 teams had presented their ideas on how to tackle some of the most interesting problems facing our government these days.
The 1st place winners had tackled the “Ethics Challenge” working in conjunction with the National Science Foundation. New federal rules state that federal employees may not receive gifts from lobbyists. This team created an app with an easy search function that would allow federal employees to quickly find out which gifts are legal. You can see the working prototype. For their prize, they will be having lunch with the Deputy Director of the National Science Foundation, Dr. Cora Marrett.
The 2nd place winners were a team of students from Thomas Jefferson High School. They tackled the CFPB Consumer Protection challenge, where they created a visualization of consumer complaints, based by-product, region or institutions. The goal is to improve the transparency and efficiency of the credit card market to further empower American consumers. You can see the graphic interface they designed. As their prize, they will be having lunch with Vint Cerf, one of the “fathers of the internet”.
The 3rd place winners tackled the EPA’s challenge to identify harmful algal blooms that have severe impacts on human health, aquatic ecosystems, and the economy. The winners found an image segmentation method called "active contours" that outlines colors in photos. They began adapting the algorithm and code to find the outline of algal blooms in water. Their prize for 3rd Place was a Tesla Coil.
